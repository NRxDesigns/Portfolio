About the project

NAB’s existing My Goals feature had strong intent but struggled in execution. The experience was constrained by outdated backend technology and the absence of a modern design system. Customers frequently reported friction, unclear behaviours and confusing layouts, which contributed to a low NPS and limited adoption.

The feature needed a complete rethink. It had to be migrated to new systems, rebuilt within NAB’s emerging design system and redesigned to genuinely support customers in saving with confidence.

Objective

Revamp the legacy experience using the new design system and re-imagine the end to end journey to address customer pain points, supported by measurable testing outcomes.

Challenges

Issue

#1 Back-end limitations

Only one savings account could be nominated as an account with Saving Spaces.

Issue

#2 Transfer limitations

Customers couldn’t move funds from any account into their Saving Spaces, it had to be from the nominated account.

Issue

#3 New design system

The new design system was still developing and many components were not yet available.

Challenging the challenges

New design system

The scale of the project meant adopting a design system that was still in its early stages. Close collaboration with the Design System team was essential. While they focused on foundational components, I contributed by gathering testing insights and refining more specialised elements, including the progress indicator. This helped strengthen the system while ensuring the experience remained cohesive and production ready.

Challenging the challenges

Back-end/transfer limitations

The single‑account constraint created a rigid experience for customers who managed money across multiple accounts. Transfers into a Saving Space were only possible from the nominated account, which meant users had to manually move money around before contributing to their goals.

To reduce this friction, I designed an automatic sorter that instantly allocated incoming funds the moment they landed in the nominated account. It was not a perfect workaround, but it gave customers a sense of control and was positively received in testing.

Prototype and testing

I created a draft end to end experience and recruited 12 participants through usertesting.com for moderated testing. The goal was to test usability and achieve exQual feedback to meet Gold targets (3.5+ out of 5).

The sessions were highly successful and the experience exceeded expectations, achieving a Platinum exQual score of 4.62 out of 5.

Findings were synthesised into a presentation and shared with the wider team.

Design to development

With testing targets achieved, I refined the work into high fidelity Figma designs. The experience was complex, with multiple pathways and error states, resulting in more than 400 frames across mobile and desktop.

I structured screens into process map style flows to clearly communicate behaviours to developers, and documented all error states in detail.

Master components were used to ensure updates cascaded consistently across every screen.

Outcomes

Launch success

Saving Spaces launched with strong results, with goal deposits exceeding expectations at $145 million.

User base adoption increase

User adoption significantly outperformed the previous My Goals feature with 10k+ more users (13% increase) and 43k+ more goals (43% increase )

Award winning

Saving Spaces won Best Digital Banking Initiative at the Global Retail Banking Innovations Awards.

Friction reduction

Friction scores improved from a baseline of 34 to 25 due to efficiencies introduced across the dashboard and transfer flows.
