First Steps

Making investing less daunting.

About

I surveyed 200 people on investing and found that more than half of adults have little to no understanding of investing, and around 73% say they want to begin but feel the process is too complex or risky. For beginners, the combination of jargon, endless product choices, and fear of making a mistake often becomes a barrier that stops them before they even start.

First Steps seeks to offer a beginner‑friendly path into investing with minimal fees, a small set of hand‑selected ETF options, and a clean, simplified interface designed to reduce anxiety.

Objective

The goal of First Steps is to get people with little to no knowledge of investing within 5 minutes by utilising their pre-existing accounts to fast track onboarding and provide learning material to get them confident enough to get started.

Understanding our customer

Workshops

I surveyed 200 participants who identified as having no knowledge or beginner‑level understanding of the stock market. The research was split into two targeted surveys of 100 participants each. The first explored emotional barriers to investing, willingness to invest, and perceptions of fees. The second examined broader investing behaviour, including past experiences, research habits of those who have invested before, and how non‑investors seek information.

Using Cursor AI, I consolidated testing notes and synthesised the findings into clear themes that accurately represented the needs and motivations of this demographic.

Creating personas and journey maps

From the research insights, I developed personas grounded in real participant sentiment and behaviour. I then created detailed journey maps using narrative storytelling and Figma‑generated illustrations to visualise each persona’s end‑to‑end experience.

The organisation responded strongly to the clarity and impact of the journeys, leading me to transform the Figma template into a .MDC file and Cursor AI command that the wider team could reuse. This meant that anyone could put in a rough persona and narrative into Cursor and it would replicate what I achieved in this journey.

Prototype

Crafting Simple Investing

Core loop and onboarding

Using the journey‑map narrative as the foundation, we defined a clear core loop for Simple Investing customers, one that guided beginners from initial curiosity to confident action. I then designed an onboarding experience streamlined enough to get customers investing within five minutes, removing friction while still giving them the context they needed to feel secure in their decisions.

Designing a simple experience

To keep the product approachable, ETFs were presented in a clean card‑based carousel, allowing customers to browse options at their own pace. They could buy and sell their ETFs through a straightforward flow, supported by a learning hub on the dashboard that helped them build knowledge over time. Once invested, customers could view their ETF holdings through a minimal, easy‑to‑understand interface that reinforced clarity and confidence throughout their journey.

Using AI to bring the journey to life

Building interactive journeys with Cursor

I noticed a recurring issue when presenting work to stakeholders: showing the prototype alone lacked the customer’s perspective, while showing the journey map lacked the depth of the prototype. To bridge this gap, I used Cursor AI to build an interactive journey that combined both views into a single experience.

Stakeholders could choose a persona with specific goals, progress through the prototype, and see real‑time reactions showing how that persona’s mood, confidence and understanding shifted at each step. It felt more like an interactive game than a static artefact, making the experience far more engaging and intuitive.

The response was overwhelmingly positive, so I turned it into a reusable tool. Other teams can now plug in their own prototypes and personas to generate interactive journeys for their projects.

Challenges

80+ onboarding questions

Due to legal requirements, customers would need to input over 80 profile questions such as name, DOB, occupation details, compliance, tax etc

$500 min investment requirement

The ASX has a $500 minimum first time investment requirement. Not great when our customer surveys showed our demographic would only be willing to put between $10 and $200 as their first investment.

Highly complex process

According to our surveys, trading on the stock market was perceived as highly complex and daunting. How were we going to make it simple and easy to understand?

Challenging the challenges

Issue

Onboarding

Solution

We streamlined the onboarding by KYCing customers upfront and reusing the data already collected during app registration. Instead of asking for information twice, we presented a single summary screen for confirmation.

This reduced the flow from more than two hundred clicks to just two, removing unnecessary friction.

Issue

$500 investment

Solution

The original five hundred dollar minimum was a major barrier, so we shifted to a custodial ownership model where we become the legal owner of the assets.

This allowed customers to bypass the minimum and start investing with as little as one dollar, making the product far more accessible.

Issue

Complex process

Solution

To simplify the experience, I embedded learning throughout the journey with tutorials, contextual help and clear explanations wherever jargon was unavoidable.

Using casual, approachable language helped customers feel supported and guided through what can otherwise be an intimidating process.

Testing the prototype

With the blueprinting for First Steps Investing complete and our draft flows in place, I took the experience into market to validate the direction. I ran twelve one hour interviews with customers to understand how well the onboarding journey, education moments and ETF selection process resonated.

Overall sentiment was very positive. Customers consistently highlighted how quick and low friction the onboarding felt. Interestingly, eight participants asked for more education, even if it meant a longer process. They wanted clearer explanations of ETFs, guidance on what to choose, how fees work and a simple breakdown of how the stock market operates. This reinforced that confidence building was just as important as speed.

We originally aimed for an approval rating of 3.5 out of 5. The reception exceeded expectations and the prototype achieved 4.82 out of 5, giving us strong validation that the core experience was on the right track.
