About the project

EPA Victoria contracted Deloitte Digital to transform their 20 year old public facing website into a modern, content authored platform.

The website suffered from cluttered navigation, slow content authoring and legacy back-end systems.

I was brought on as the sole user experience designer to re-imagine the site in 8 weeks.

Approach

Given the time frame of eight weeks and the fact development would begin three weeks after I started, I needed to make key decisions to get the project moving.

The project was broken up into 3 parts:

  • 3 weeks building a foundational design system and website concepts
  • 2 weeks designing AirWatch
  • 3 weeks refining the design system

The designs were validated by conducting frequent workshops with a broad range of SMEs and guerrilla user testing for user feedback.

Design System

Fast tracking the system

Build was scheduled to start 3 weeks after I rolled onto the project so time was of the essence.

Through early stakeholder workshops, I uncovered that Vic Gov were creating a Single Digital Presence design system, known as Ripple, was in the early stages of development.

Rather than starting from scratch, I proactively connected with the Ripple team to understand their progress and identify opportunities to leverage their work to accelerate EPA Victoria’s delivery.

Design System

Auditing & Structuring Our Own ‘Ripple’

The Ripple team provided work-in-progress Sketch files, which were unstructured and in the ideation phase.

To make these assets usable within the deadline, I rapidly audited and dissected the designs into an atomic structure. I chose an atomic design approach deliberately, as the primary goal was to create a component and template repository that content authors could easily reuse.

Design System

Building the Design System

At the outset, the design system was partially formed, missing key components and lacking high-fidelity polish. While refining foundational atoms, I ran hands-on workshops (3 times a week) with content authors and subject matter experts (SMEs) to understand what information they required to display. From here, I was able to devise common components that became the molecules and organisms of the design system.

Content authors and SMEs actively contributed requirements, and I translated those needs into flexible, reusable components. Due to the short timelines, component requests had to be prioritised.

We did this by evaluating based on assumed frequency of use, validated during workshops, ensuring the system delivered immediate value.

Design System

Templates & Governance

Once the majority of components were established, I defined the boundaries of page templates. Five core templates were created.

Each template included clear layout rules, supporting single- and double-column configurations, providing structure while maintaining flexibility for content authors.

Delivery & Hand Off

Using Zeplin, I documented and handed off components to the development team, enabling rapid implementation within Sitecore. We sat shoulder to shoulder on a daily basis to keep communication constant. This approach allowed:

  • Developers to build core components quickly
  • Content authors to begin creating pages early
  • Continuous feedback to be captured and folded back into the evolving design system via weekly sessions with content authors

EPA AirWatch

Objective and customer problem

One of the biggest values of EPA Vic is their monitoring of air quality across Victoria and is a vital resource for those that suffer from respiratory illnesses. The feature, AirWatch, needed to be reimagined as part of the transformation.

The main issue was the information provided was hidden and too scientific for the general public.

The tricky part, we had two weeks and there were strict rules to abide by such as the air quality forecasts needing to be understood by children in the fifth grade.

EPA AirWatch

Refresh process

Workshops

Between 4 large workshops of 20 stakeholders across the AirWatch space, I was able to develop a rating system and clean UI to surface air quality readings in a simplistic way using words like ‘good and ‘poor’ and traffic light colours.

Challenge arises

While the system proved a success with the general public, another demographic that consisted of scientists found it lacking. They needed specific data readings of each air pollution type in numeric formats which this new system did not cater for. I designed a separate data focused interface that could be access via the landing page (to not violate our reading level requirement) for this group which led to successful engagement.

Outcomes

Design system completed

In just 8 weeks, the design system was integrated with SiteCore and allowed content authors to quickly build the new site within weeks.

During the 19/20 bushfires, AirWatch played a vital role allowing the public to take health measures against heavy smoke pollutants.
